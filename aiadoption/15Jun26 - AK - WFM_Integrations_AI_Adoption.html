Correlation verdict

S1→S2: AI usage jumped from 85% → 97% and epics closed surged (0 → 5). Strong positive signal — AI ramp-up correlated directly with delivery acceleration.

S2→S3: AI rate dropped 24pp (97% → 73%) and epic closures slowed significantly (5 → 8, only 3 more). Moderate negative signal.

S3→S4: AI SP delivery has collapsed to 1% with zero epics closed so far. The correlation is stark — this is not a sprint-end lag issue, it is a behavioral regression in AI tool usage that is almost certainly impacting throughput. The pattern is directionally consistent across all sprints: when AI usage drops, delivery drops.

Summary for management

The correlation between AI usage and epic delivery is directionally positive across PI-2: the sprint with the highest AI SP delivery rate (S2, 97%) also saw the highest burst of epic closures (5 epics). The current S4 collapse to 1% AI SP delivered — with zero epics closed so far — continues that pattern in the wrong direction.

The three highest-priority items: (1) Eureka has used AI well but cannot close epics — investigate epic sizing and definition of done. (2) Titans has used AI well but closed zero epics all PI and has gone dark on AI in S4 — this team needs a 1:1 this week. (3) The S4 AI usage collapse is not isolated to one team — it's a systemic drop across Horizon, Titans, Odyssey, and Eureka simultaneously, which suggests a tooling issue, a labeling gap, or a behavioral regression that needs a direct conversation with all teams before the sprint ends.
