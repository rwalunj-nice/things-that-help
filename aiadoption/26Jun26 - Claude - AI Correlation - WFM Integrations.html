How is Trend r calculated?
Trend r (Pearson correlation coefficient) measures how consistently a team's AI adoption % moves in one direction over sprints. It ranges from −1 (perfect downward line) through 0 (no pattern) to +1 (perfect upward line).

Worked example — Sinhagad (r = 0.94):
We correlate sprint index (x = 1, 2, 3, 4) against AI% (y = 29, 63, 79, 83):
  Means: x̄ = 2.5, ȳ = 63.5
  For each sprint, multiply (x−x̄) × (y−ȳ) → sum = +81
  Normalize: r = 81 / √5 × √1877 = 81 / 86.06 ≈ +0.94
Because AI% rose almost every sprint, the deviations multiply to large positive values → r near +1.

What does r tell us?
r ≥ 0.70 Consistent upward trend — structural adoption, likely to sustain
r 0.30–0.69 Gradual rise — positive but inconsistent, needs reinforcement
r −0.29 to +0.29 No net trend — spikes cancel drops, volatile or flat
r ≤ −0.30 Declining — adoption peaking and falling back, needs intervention
