## Key Findings Across All Frameworks

### Finding 1: Budget Crisis Is the #1 Team Issue

All 4 frameworks identify the same budget crisis group. The top 5 premium consumers (nilesht-19, thakraln, trunalgawade, Prathmesh-Ranadive, PradnyeshSalunke) consumed ~65,708 premium requests. At current team rates, this is approximately 25-30% of total team premium budget generating less than 15% of total LoC. CRQC makes this most explicit through the R=0 override rule.

### Finding 2: Two Tier A Efficiency Collapses Require Intervention

mshnayderman (−88% ReqEff) and amol-salunkhe (−84% ReqEff) both entered this period as strong Tier A performers and exited with 9.5× and 7× premium spikes respectively. CRQC correctly demotes both to Tier C. v1/v2 retain Tier A based on output volume, which is appropriate for team-level reporting but not for coaching conversations about ROI.

**Recommended approach:** Report as "Tier A by output, Tier C by ROI" in the executive scorecard. Both need immediate investigation into what changed in their workflow between June 3 and June 8.

### Finding 3: Two Genuine Breakout Stories

rpawar-nice (+199% ReqEff) and Kranti-nice (+204% ReqEff) are unambiguous coaching success stories. The Role+Momentum framework captures this most explicitly. Both started with below-par or borderline metrics on June 3 and are now clear Tier A contributors. Document the coaching actions that drove these improvements.

### Finding 4: CRQC Reveals Hidden Value in Several "Tier D/E" Users

Users like sskalaskar, prashasti-jain, moadzughul, sachinfuse-nice, pdevle, and tusharpatil166719 score 5-7 in CRQC despite appearing in v1 Tier D/E. The CRQC result suggests these users have reasonable efficiency and quality signals — their v1 D/E classification is driven by **low absolute LoC volume**, not poor efficiency. Coaching recommendation: these users don't need a fundamentally different approach, they need to increase usage frequency/scale.
